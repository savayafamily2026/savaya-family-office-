Governance

Rigorous governance is not a constraint on the family's ambition — it is the architecture that makes perpetual ambition possible.

Governance Philosophy

Structure preserves what judgment alone cannot.

SAVAYA's governance framework exists to institutionalise good decision-making — not to replace the principal's judgment, but to ensure that judgment operates within a structure designed to outlast any single generation.

Four interlocking pillars — an Investment Policy Statement, a deliberating Investment Committee, a formal Risk Management programme, and a Succession framework — form the structural foundation. Each one reinforces the others.

"Governance is the difference between wealth that lasts a generation and wealth that lasts a century."

Four Pillars

The architecture of perpetual stewardship.

Four interlocking governance pillars, each designed to reinforce the others.

01

Investment Policy Statement

The IPS is SAVAYA's central governance document — binding all capital decisions to a principled framework, reviewed annually, and resistant to market noise or short-term pressure.

Formalises long-term capital objectives and risk tolerance
Defines asset class policy ranges with upper and lower bounds
Binds all advisors and family members to one framework
02

Investment Committee

Structured deliberation for all material allocation decisions — ensuring conviction, documentation, and IPS alignment before capital moves.

Quarterly portfolio review with formal minutes and resolutions
Approval authority for investments above policy thresholds
Independent advisory participation on major decisions
03

Risk Management

Concentration, currency, counterparty, and operational risks are monitored continuously — with pre-agreed protocols and quarterly stress testing against macro scenarios.

Concentration limits at investment, sector, and geography
Quarterly stress testing against macro and geopolitical scenarios
Pre-agreed currency hedging and counterparty due diligence
04

Succession & Continuity

Succession planning is not deferred — it is embedded from the outset. Clear authority protocols, trust structures, and next-generation induction ensure continuity of purpose, not just capital.

Documented authority succession with contingency protocols
Trust structures aligned with multi-jurisdictional estate planning
Next-generation governance induction and education
Investment Policy Statement

Capital that outlasts its stewards.

The Investment Policy Statement is governance in writing. It encodes the family's objectives, risk discipline, and decision-making authority in a single binding document — one that outlasts any market cycle, any steward, and any moment of pressure.

Perpetual Horizon

No terminal date. Individual positions held for a decade or more. Real return target of 5–7% per annum across full market cycles.

Embedded Risk Discipline

Maximum 25% peak-to-trough drawdown. Minimum 20% in assets realisable within 90 days at all times. Pre-agreed rebalancing and hedging protocols.

Living Governance

Reviewed annually. Binding on all advisors and family members. Amended following any material change in family circumstances, regulatory environment, or market structure.

Governance that earns trust before capital is committed.
