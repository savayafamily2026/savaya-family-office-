01Artificial Intelligence · Global Infrastructure

AI & Machine Intelligence

Artificial intelligence has become the defining capital cycle of this decade, cumulative global spend on AI-specific infrastructure is projected to exceed USD 5 trillion by 2030. We invest across the value chain to capture a technology shift we believe will restructure how every industry computes, decides, and operates.

We believe enduring value will accrue to businesses that provide critical infrastructure, proprietary data and intelligent systems capable of translating AI capabilities into measurable productivity.

02Energy · Nuclear, Solar & Hydrogen

New Sources of Energy

Global investment in renewables, nuclear, grids and related technologies reached approximately $2.2 trillion in 2025, twice the capital directed towards fossil fuels. We invest in nuclear, solar, and hydrogen — the technologies redefining how the world generates, stores, and distributes power.

03Financial Technology · Capital Markets Infrastructure

Financial Technology & Digital Finance

We invest in the software rewriting the rules of finance. As embedded finance, stablecoins, and AI displace legacy banking rails, we back the companies rebuilding payments, lending, and capital markets from first principles.

04Infrastructure · Real Estate · Robotics

Infrastructure, Real Estate & Robotics

We invest in essential physical assets and the technologies that make them more productive—including logistics, digital infrastructure, real estate and automation. An estimated $20 trillion financing gap remains through 2040 — a shortfall only long-duration private capital can close. Within this thesis, we are building a deliberate and growing position in robotics.

Investment Approach

Capital is committed to structural change, not market cycles.

Savaya Capital does not trade themes. Where we identify a durable structural transformation, we commit capital over horizons of a decade or more, building concentrated, high-conviction positions in which we hold a clear informational or relational advantage.
